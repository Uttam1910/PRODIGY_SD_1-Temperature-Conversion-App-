About Temperature Scales

Temperature is a measure of the warmth or coldness of an object or environment. It is measured in various scales:

  • Celsius (°C): Used in most of the world for everyday temperature measurements.
  • Fahrenheit (°F): Mainly used in the United States for weather and cooking temperatures.
  • Kelvin (K): Used in scientific contexts, especially in physics and chemistry.
